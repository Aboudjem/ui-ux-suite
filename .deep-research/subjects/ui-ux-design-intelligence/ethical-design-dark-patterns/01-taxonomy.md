# Dark Pattern Taxonomy & Classification

## Brignull's 16 Types (deceptive.design)

Harry Brignull coined "dark patterns" in 2010 (darkpatterns.org, now deceptive.design). His 2023 book "Deceptive Patterns" formalized 16 types:

1. **Comparison Prevention** -- features/prices combined in complex manner, essential info hard to find
2. **Confirmshaming** -- emotionally manipulating users into actions through guilt or shame
3. **Disguised Ads** -- interface elements that look like native content but are advertisements
4. **Fake Scarcity** -- fake indication of limited supply or popularity
5. **Fake Social Proof** -- fake reviews, testimonials, or activity messages
6. **Fake Urgency** -- fake time limitations pressuring action
7. **Forced Action** -- requiring undesirable action to complete desired task
8. **Hard to Cancel** -- easy signup, difficult cancellation (roach motel)
9. **Hidden Costs** -- unexpected fees revealed after time investment
10. **Hidden Subscription** -- unknowing enrollment in recurring payments
11. **Nagging** -- persistent interruptions requesting alternative actions
12. **Obstruction** -- barriers making tasks or information access difficult
13. **Preselection** -- default options pre-selected to influence decisions
14. **Sneaking** -- pertinent information hidden or delayed in transactions
15. **Trick Wording** -- confusing or misleading language causing unintended actions
16. **Visual Interference** -- information hidden, obscured, or disguised against expectations

## 68-Type Academic Taxonomy (2024)

A comprehensive study expanded classification to 68 types across 6 main categories:

| Category | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Nagging | Persistent interruption requests | Repeated permission prompts, upgrade nudges |
| Obstruction | Barriers to user goals | Complex cancellation, hidden settings |
| Sneaking | Hidden information/actions | Hidden costs, auto-enrollment |
| Interface Interference | Visual/interaction manipulation | Prominence tricks, pre-checked boxes |
| Forced Action | Required undesirable actions | Forced registration, social sharing |
| Social Engineering | Exploiting social/emotional biases | Confirmshaming, fake social proof |

Key finding: 72% of patterns create multi-faceted impacts across financial loss (H2), privacy violations (H3), and trust erosion (H6).

## CHI 2024 Ontology (64 synthesized types)

Synthesized 10 existing regulatory and academic taxonomies into a three-level ontology:
- **Low-level**: Specific implementation patterns (e.g., "pre-checked opt-in checkbox")
- **Meso-level**: Pattern categories (e.g., "preselection", "visual interference")
- **High-level**: Strategic goals (e.g., "subverting user autonomy", "extracting data")

## Instagram Dark Pattern Evolution (CHI 2025)

Longitudinal analysis of Instagram 2010-2024 identified 4 new deceptive patterns:
- Shopping tab placed where notification heart button was (muscle memory exploitation)
- Gradual introduction of manipulative patterns over time prevents user backlash
- Demonstrates how platforms use incremental dark pattern deployment
