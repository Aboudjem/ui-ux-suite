# Regulatory Landscape: Dark Pattern Enforcement

## EU: Digital Services Act (DSA)

### Article 25 -- Dark Pattern Prohibition
- **Effective**: February 17, 2024 (majority of DSA provisions)
- **Scope**: First EU law to explicitly describe and prohibit "dark patterns" on online platforms
- **Prohibition**: Platforms may not design, organize, or operate interfaces using dark patterns that materially distort or impair user autonomy, decision-making, or choice

### Early Enforcement Cases
- **Amazon Prime**: Cancellation flow investigated, settlement reached regarding manipulative UI
- **Eventim**: Higher Regional Court of Bamberg ruled consumer org could not rely on Article 25(1) alone against insurance nudging on ticketing site -- showing enforcement boundaries still being defined

### Digital Fairness Act (Expected Q4 2026)
- Aims to consolidate fragmented dark pattern regulation across DSA, DMA, Data Act, AI Act, and EmpCo Directive
- Risk of inconsistencies leading to under-enforcement (Inge Graef warning)
- Legislative proposal expected from Commission to Parliament and Council

### Digital Omnibus (Nov 2025)
- Commission admits cookie consent fix is "long overdue"
- Proposes Article 88b: machine-readable consent signals from browsers
- NOYB and BEUC warn it doesn't mandate reject-as-default

## US: Federal Trade Commission (FTC)

### Enforcement Actions 2024-2026
| Case | Year | Outcome |
|------|------|---------|
| Amazon Prime | 2025 | $2.5B settlement (largest ever dark pattern case) |
| Adobe | 2024 | Complaint filed for hidden cancellation fees |
| ABCmouse | 2024 | $10M consumer refunds ordered |
| Vonage | 2024 | $100M consumer refunds required |

Since January 2025 alone: 5 new cases initiated, 6 settlements approved for negative option misconduct.

### Click-to-Cancel Rule
- **Finalized**: October 16, 2024
- **Requirement**: Cancellation must be as easy as enrollment
- **Vacated**: July 8, 2025 by 8th Circuit Court of Appeals (failure to complete required regulatory analysis under Section 22 of FTC Act)
- **Current status**: FTC restarted subscription rulemaking in March 2026
- **Still enforceable**: ROSCA, FTC Act Section 5, state auto-renewal laws, Visa/Mastercard subscription standards

### ICPEN Global Sweep (2024)
- Examined 642 websites and mobile apps across 26 countries
- **76%** used at least one dark pattern
- **67%** used multiple dark patterns
- Focus on subscription services and privacy settings

## US: California (CPRA/CCPA)

### Definition
"A user interface designed or manipulated with the substantial effect of subverting or impairing user autonomy, decisionmaking, or choice."

### Key Regulations
- Symmetrical choices required: "yes/no" is symmetric; "yes/ask me later" is not
- Easy-to-read language for consumer disclosures
- Consent obtained through dark patterns is void

### Enforcement
- CPPA Enforcement Advisory issued September 4, 2024 -- signaling active enforcement priority
- Fines: $2,500 per violation; $7,500 if intentional
- CHI 2025 study found systematic dark patterns in CCPA opt-out processes across websites

## Emerging Regulatory Areas

### Insurance (India - IRDAI, 2026)
- Pre-ticked add-ons and hidden renewal tweaks targeted
- Dark pattern regulation expanding beyond tech to financial services

### Global Convergence
- Pattern recognition across jurisdictions: subscription traps, consent manipulation, deceptive cancellation
- Enforcement gap between law and implementation remains significant
