# Subscription UX: Sign-up vs Cancellation Parity

## The Asymmetry Problem

The core dark pattern in subscriptions is **asymmetrical effort**: easy to join, deliberately hard to leave.

### ICPEN 2024 Global Sweep Results
- **642** websites and mobile apps examined across **26 countries**
- **76%** used at least one dark pattern
- **67%** used multiple dark patterns

### Most Common Cancellation Dark Patterns
1. **Loss Aversion Manipulation** -- found in 5/6 services studied
2. **Emotional Exploitation** -- guilt-tripping language ("Are you sure? You'll lose all your progress")
3. **Hidden Options** -- cancellation buried deep in settings
4. **Navigation Barriers** -- multi-step processes, phone-only cancellation

## Landmark Enforcement

### Amazon Prime ($2.5B Settlement, Sept 2025)
- Largest dark pattern enforcement action in history
- $1B in penalties + $1.5B in consumer refunds
- Must clearly disclose subscription terms before collecting billing info
- Must remove manipulative enrollment and cancellation flows

### Adobe (FTC Complaint, June 2024)
- Failed to clearly disclose "Annual, Paid Monthly" plan terms
- Made cancelling subscriptions unfairly difficult
- Early termination fee buried in fine print

### Other Cases
- ABCmouse: $10M refunds, required to simplify cancellation
- Vonage: $100M refunds to affected customers

## The Click-to-Cancel Rule

### Timeline
- **Oct 2024**: FTC finalizes rule -- cancellation must be as easy as enrollment
- **July 2025**: 8th Circuit vacates rule (insufficient regulatory analysis)
- **March 2026**: FTC restarts subscription rulemaking

### Still Enforceable Under
- ROSCA (Restore Online Shoppers' Confidence Act)
- FTC Act Section 5 (unfair or deceptive practices)
- State auto-renewal laws (California, New York, etc.)
- Visa and Mastercard subscription standards

## Ethical Subscription UX Principles

### Parity Requirements
1. **Same channel**: If signup is online, cancellation must be online
2. **Same effort**: Same number of clicks/steps for both
3. **Clear pricing**: All costs disclosed upfront, including renewal terms
4. **No save flows that obstruct**: Retention offers are OK; blocking cancellation is not
5. **Confirmation without manipulation**: Confirm cancellation without guilt language

### Implementation Checklist
- [ ] Cancellation link visible in account settings (not buried)
- [ ] Maximum 3 clicks from account page to confirmed cancellation
- [ ] No phone-only cancellation if signup was digital
- [ ] No "dark nagging" (repeated save offers after user chose to cancel)
- [ ] Clear disclosure of what access remains until billing period ends
- [ ] Confirmation email with resubscription option (not auto-re-enrollment)
